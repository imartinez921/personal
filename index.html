  • Why did I quit my last job?
    The pandemic was a crucial time for all of us. While some of the workforce began to WFH, as a pharmacist & pharmacy manager for Walgreens I never stopped working. When the COVID-19 vaccines were approved at the end of 2020, I spent the next 5 months feeling like I was saving the world. But at the same time, I was in survival mode â€“ skipping my lunch breaks, working up to 16h a day to make a dent in the workload.... There were simply too many people relying on me; even if I had to work alone, I showed up anyway. I asked corporate for more hands on deck or a modified schedule, but I didn't stop or slow down when aid wasn't given to me.... Then in March 2021, when I was scheduled to work 11 days in a row, I finally had a panic attack at work on the 9th day. I told myself that I would wait for it to happen again before I would consider leaving my job, but in the months following, I began making mistakes in the prescriptions I was checking. I had to come to terms with the fact that I was here to help people, but because I was burned out & not mentally well, my next mistake could hurt someone. I resigned 2 months later, in June 2021.
  • But why did I leave pharmacy all together?
    Turning 29 after my resignation was a true reckoning for me. Not only was I doing therapy to help me understand my exhaustion & anxiety, but I was coming to terms with the fact that I had always lived my entire life for others, and it would kill me if I continued. I resolved that my first step to learning how to assert boundaries would be never putting myself in that kind of all-sacrificing position again. Unfortunately, this meant that I didn't see a future for myself in the U.S. healthcare system, especially because my sole motivation in pharmacy had been talking and teaching patients.
  • So how did I find software engineering?
    It has been an emotional, meaningful journey to get here. My therapist asked me to think of what came naturally to me as a child (before I had internalized an identity), and what came to mind was writing & fiddling with computers. Before high school, I was obsessed with getting the right fonts and colors for the song lyrics on my Myspace page. I would procrastinate on my pre-pharmacy studies for hours just because my Tumblr theme didn't look exactly how I wanted it. When my 2011 Macbook Pro started failing during pharmacy school, I replaced the hard drive with an SSD. (This ultimately ended up being the computer I completed bootcamp with!) Unfortunately, no one at that time ever told me that computers or technology could be a career. Fortunately, the pandemic had revealed SWE as a booming field, and I had many SWE friends who encouraged me that I could do this. In April 2022, I decided to sit in for App Academy's pre-bootcamp (for ground zero learners), knowing I could leave any day in the first week.... But I never did. I enrolled in their in-person cohort a month later and graduated the day after my 30th birthday in September 2022.
  • How am I doing now?
    Doing well! With the market as bad as it has been since I graduated, I decided to bide my time as I recovered from the intensity of bootcamp. Nowadays, I code upwards of 6-8 hours a day at home, sometimes group projects and sometimes solo projects, while still taking time for self-reflection and self-care. I find myself able to handle much more stress than I did before bootcamp, and I'm excited to start working again in 2024. I have a pipe dream of working for a company whose mission is creating technology that will help healthcare providers take better care of their patients & daily workload (ask me in an interview how technology impacted my work as a frontline worker during the pandemic ðŸ« ) but I am open to any opportunity that will help me grow as a developer.
